Photo Retouching Cost & Rates (2026 Guide)

If you're comparing photo retouching services, the first question is always the same: how much does it cost, and what are typical retouching rates? The honest answer is that photo retouching prices range from under $1 to $30+ per image — it depends entirely on the type of editing, complexity and volume. Here's a clear 2026 breakdown of photo retouching rates and prices.

Photo retouching rates: per image vs per hour

There are two ways retouching is priced. Per-image rates (used by most studios and outsourcing partners) give you a predictable price per photo — from under $1 for a clipping path to a few dollars for product work — so you always know your cost before you order. Per-hour retoucher rates are common with individual freelancers and range from roughly $25 to $150+ per hour depending on skill and market. For e-commerce catalogues, per-image pricing is almost always cheaper and easier to budget, because you only pay for finished images, not time.

What actually affects the price?

  • Complexity — a simple cutout costs far less than multi-path jewelry with reflections.
  • Volume — bulk orders and monthly retainers get the lowest per-image rate.
  • Turnaround — rush delivery adds a premium.
  • Revisions — studios offering unlimited revisions price slightly higher (but save you headaches).

In-house vs outsourcing

A full-time retoucher can cost $2,000–$5,000+/month plus software and management. Outsourcing to a studio converts that into a predictable per-image or retainer cost — you only pay for what you need, and you can scale up for campaigns without hiring. For most brands and photographers, outsourcing is dramatically cheaper than an in-house retoucher's salary and rates.

How to get the best value

  • Start with a free test edit to judge quality before paying.
  • Ask about volume & monthly retainer rates.
  • Choose a studio that gives consistent, on-brand results — re-editing cheap work costs more in the long run.

FAQ

What are typical photo retouching rates?

Typical photo retouching rates run from about $0.50–$4 per image for e-commerce work (background removal, clipping path, product and ghost mannequin), and $10–$30+ per image for high-end beauty and editorial. Bulk and monthly-retainer rates are lower per image.

How much does it cost to retouch a product photo?

E-commerce product retouching typically costs $1–$4 per image depending on complexity, with volume discounts. Simple background removal can be under $1.

How much does a photo retoucher charge per hour?

Freelance retoucher rates range from roughly $25 to $150+ per hour depending on skill and market. For predictable pricing most e-commerce brands choose per-image rates instead, which start under $1 per image at a studio.

Is outsourcing photo retouching cheaper than hiring?

Yes, for most brands. A full-time retoucher costs thousands per month; outsourcing converts that into a per-image or retainer cost so you only pay for what you use.
