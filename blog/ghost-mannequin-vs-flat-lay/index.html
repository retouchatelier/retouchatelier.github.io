Ghost Mannequin vs Flat Lay vs On-Model

Choosing how to photograph apparel for your store comes down to three popular styles: ghost mannequin, flat lay and on-model. Each sells differently. Here's how they compare — and how post-production makes each look premium.

1. Ghost mannequin (invisible mannequin)

The garment is shot on a mannequin, then the mannequin is edited out and the inner neckline rebuilt — giving a clean, 3D “worn” look with no distraction.

  • Best for: apparel catalogs, Amazon, Shopify
  • Pros: shows true shape & fit, consistent, scalable
  • Cons: needs ghost mannequin editing to look right

2. Flat lay

The product is laid flat and shot from above. Fast and cheap to produce, great for accessories and styled sets.

  • Best for: accessories, basics, social content
  • Pros: quick, low cost, creative styling
  • Cons: doesn't show fit or drape as well

3. On-model

Worn by a real model — the most aspirational and highest-converting for fashion, but the most expensive to produce.

  • Best for: fashion brands, campaigns, lifestyle
  • Pros: emotional, shows movement & fit
  • Cons: costly; needs skin & garment retouching

Which should you use?

Most stores use a mix: ghost mannequin for the main catalog (clean & consistent), flat lay for accessories and quick drops, and on-model for hero/campaign shots. Whatever you choose, consistent retouching is what makes a catalog look professional and trustworthy.

FAQ

Is ghost mannequin better than flat lay?

They serve different goals. Ghost mannequin shows true garment shape and fit (best for apparel catalogs), while flat lay is faster and cheaper (best for accessories and social). Most stores use both.

Does ghost mannequin photography need editing?

Yes — the mannequin must be removed and the inner neckline/cuffs reconstructed in post-production to achieve the clean 3D look.

Which converts best for fashion?

On-model is usually the most aspirational and highest-converting for fashion, but it's the most expensive. Ghost mannequin is the best-value catalog standard.
